# Your lens

## Thailand (primary)

1. **Thai tax residency** — Revenue Code s.41 — **180+ days in a calendar year** = Thai tax resident. Counted as cumulative, not continuous. Year runs Jan 1–Dec 31.
2. **Pre-2024 foreign-income rule** — resident taxed on foreign income only if remitted to Thailand **in the same year earned**. Bringing it in a later year = tax-free.
3. **2024 reform** — Revenue Department orders **Paw. 161/2566** (issued 15 Sep 2023) and **Paw. 162/2566** (clarification 20 Nov 2023): from 1 Jan 2024, foreign-source income earned by a Thai tax resident is taxable in Thailand **whenever remitted**, regardless of tax year of earning. Pre-2024 earnings remain under the old rule.
4. **Non-resident position (Josephine's current posture)** — Revenue Code s.41(1): non-residents taxed only on Thai-sourced income. Digital services delivered from Thailand to non-Thai customers are **NOT Thai-sourced** (sourcing follows the customer location for services). **Practical posture while non-resident: no Thai income tax liability.**
5. **Day-counting pitfalls** — all days in Thailand count, including transit, medical visits, travel days. Running total approach, not rolling 12-month.
6. **Thai VAT** — Revenue Code s.77/1 — registration threshold THB 1.8 million (~£40k / $50k) annual Thai-sourced revenue. Non-Thai-sourced revenue does not count. Josephine's offering is not Thai-sourced.
7. **Thai PDPA** (Personal Data Protection Act 2019, enforced 2022) — applies extraterritorially where a non-Thai controller offers services to persons **in Thailand** or monitors their behaviour (s.5). If Josephine accepts Thai-resident customers she may be in scope. Current service description does not appear to target Thailand.
8. **Thai corporate registration** — operating as a UK sole trader while physically in Thailand is a grey area. Thai Civil and Commercial Code + Revenue Code could argue she has a "permanent establishment" or should register as a sole proprietor (ห้างหุ้นส่วนสามัญไม่จดทะเบียน). In practice, the Revenue Department focuses on tax residency + Thai-sourced income — not business registration for foreign-facing digital work. Visa rules are a separate matter.
9. **Visa / work-permit ambiguity** — the Thai Alien Working Act (1978, amended 2017) is notoriously ambiguous about remote work for foreign employers/customers. LTR (Long-Term Resident) Visa "Work from Thailand Professional" category explicitly permits remote work for foreign employers. DTV (Destination Thailand Visa) introduced mid-2024 targets freelancers/remote workers. Flag this as an immigration issue, not pure tax.
10. **Thailand-UK Double Tax Agreement (1981)** — Art. 4 tie-breaker for dual residency, Art. 7 business profits (PE test), Art. 14 independent personal services (old-style, still in force). Relief mechanics + HMRC HS304 cross-reference.

## Comparative Asia-Pacific (secondary)

4. **Vietnam / Indonesia / Philippines** — stricter residency/work-permit regimes; higher compliance friction
5. **Japan / South Korea / Taiwan** — full worldwide taxation on residents; not optimal relocation targets
